ROTARACT CLUB

Chartered in 2013 as the first student activity group at National School of Business Management, Rotaract Club of NSBM has acquired a significant membership of over 100 capable youth leaders representing diverse areas of discipline such as Management, Computing and Interior Design, and has initiated a portfolio of significant projects ever since. These projects include ‘Flair’ — an acoustic night unleashing true talent, ‘Manusathkara’ — a series of health camps conducted for under-privileged communities, offering free eye-checkups and free spectacles to elderly people , ‘Mentor’ — a career guidance open forum done in collaboration with Uniliver, Sri Lanka, Christmas Carols and many more. In its history of six years thus far, the club has already marked its name as a strong and vibrant Rotaract club in the RI District 3220 – Sri Lanka & Maldives, with a number of notable achievements and awards, and leaders gifted to the community at large. It is sponsored by one of the strongest Rotary clubs in the District — the Rotary Club of Colombo West.

AIESEC

AIESEC is the world’s largest youth run organization present in 124 countries. We develop leadership skills of youth through cross-cultural exchange programs. AIESEC provides a global platform for young people to explore and develop their leadership potential. AIESEC in NSBM was officially installed in January 2017 as an Initiative Group directly under AIESEC Sri Lanka, within a very short time span upgraded to an Official Expansion and awarded as the “Most Progressive Expansion” for two consecutive years. As one of the most active societies in NSBM, our AIESECers take multiple initiatives throughout the year to ensure the development of its’ membership body. Within a year we embark approximately 15 Incoming Global Volunteer projects, bring in more than 100 Exchange Participants. While giving our own graduates the opportunity to become a Global Volunteer for multiple projects initiated in different countries.



TOASTMASTER CLUB

Toastmasters is an educational organization that operates clubs worldwide for the purpose of promoting communication and public speaking skills. Toastmasters uses a club-based structure, each having around 20–40 members. Meetings are held every week and usually in the evening. Part of meetings is devoted to Table Topics, which are off-the-cuff speeches which are assigned on the spot by a Topics master. The goal of this is to think on one’s feet with minimal preparation. Every meeting is also based around a set of organized speeches. Speakers are evaluated by an experienced member who then gives an impromptu speech with constructive feedback based on their performance.
